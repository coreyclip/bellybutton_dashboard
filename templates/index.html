Lables are the Lowest taxonomic level of bacteria and archaea identified (‘phylotype’ or OTU, operational taxonomic unit) in the samples. Taxonomies reflect the lowest taxonomic level to which samples could be given a name. If samples were so different from existing samples as to not readily be classified they are simply labeled "bacteria" reflecting that they were different from everything we know. Taxonomies presented follow the taxonomic rank you learned about in Intro Biology: Kingdom (or Domain in the case of Archaea); Phylum; Subphylum; Order; Family; Genus. See Hulcr et al. 2012 for specific methods. Values indicate sequencing read numbers.
